The Technology Adoption Curve, Twenty Years On

Two decades of software's biggest ideas, plotted from the frontier to the mainstream on the bell curve that explains how every technology spreads.

The same pattern repeats with every new technology: a handful of innovators try it first, early adopters follow, and the rest of the industry arrives only once it is proven. Watch where an idea sits on that curve and you can read its future.

01

One curve, eight shifts

Everett Rogers split any new technology's audience into five groups by how soon they adopt. Plotting two decades of major ideas onto that curve shows something quietly useful: the newer the technology, the further left it sits. Today's frontier is yesterday's air we breathe.
